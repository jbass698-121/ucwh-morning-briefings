March Federal Deadlines Will Reshape AI Regulation

Baker Botts / King & Spalding · Mar 2026 · HIGH

Three critical deadlines hit this month under Trump's December 2025 executive order on AI. By March 11, the FTC must issue a policy statement on how antitrust law applies to AI, including when state laws requiring alteration of truthful AI outputs are preempted. The Commerce Department must publish an evaluation of burdensome state AI laws by the same date. By March 16, the FCC must explore federal reporting standards for AI models that would preempt state requirements. The Colorado AI Act remains on track for June 30.

Why this matters for you: The March 11 FTC deadline is the one to watch — it will clarify whether the federal government is going to actively preempt state-by-state AI regulation. If you're deploying AI products across states, the compliance landscape could simplify dramatically or fragment further depending on these rulings.
