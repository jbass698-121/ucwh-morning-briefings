🔵 Mister Car Wash

Q4 2025 Results: $1.05B Annual Revenue, 2.3M Members — No 2026 Guidance

Financial

Mister Car Wash reported Q4 2025 net revenues of $261.2M (+4% YoY) and full-year 2025 revenue of $1.052B — crossing the $1B milestone for the first time. The Unlimited Wash Club grew to approximately 2.3 million members (+7% YoY, +147K members), with UWC sales now representing 79% of total wash sales (up from 75% in Q4 2024). Adjusted EBITDA for 2025 increased 8% to $345.4M. Comp-store sales grew 1.6% in Q4. Notably, MCW will not provide 2026 guidance and canceled its earnings call due to the pending take-private transaction.

Sources: ir.mistercarwash.com (2/18), StockTitan, carwash.org

Take-Private Legal Pressure Continues — Investigation Notices Still Active

Legal

The shareholder law firm investigations into the $7.00/share Leonard Green take-private deal remain active through March 2026. Kessler Topaz, Halper Sadeh, Robbins Geller, and others continue examining whether the buyout price adequately compensates minority shareholders, noting the offer is ~20% below MCW's 52-week high. No court injunctions have been filed, and the $3.1B deal remains on track for H1 2026 close. MCW expanded its portfolio to 548 locations (16 greenfield + 5 acquired in 2025) but post-close visibility into operations will disappear as MCW goes private.

Sources: GlobeNewsWire (3/16), PRNewswire, Latham & Watkins, Yahoo Finance

📊 Industry Trends & Market Data

Global Car Wash Market: $36.3B → $54.5B by 2033, Subscription + Eco-Friendly Lead Growth

Market Data

A major market research report published March 20 projects the global car wash market will grow from $36.29B (2025) to $54.48B by 2033 at a 5.1% CAGR. Two forces drive the growth: subscription/membership models (projected to advance at 10.42% CAGR through 2031, with top platforms converting 35% of visitors into unlimited members) and eco-friendly solutions (water recycling, biodegradable products driven by tightening regulations). The US express exterior format now commands over 55% of the domestic $15.8B market. This long-term trajectory validates UCWH's membership-first approach even as near-term saturation creates competitive headwinds.

Sources: GlobeNewsWire (3/20), Persistence Market Research, Grand View Research

ICA Q1 2026 Pulse Update: Stable Demand, Price Sensitivity Rising Alongside Saturation

Market Data

The ICA Q1 2026 CAR WASH Pulse report continues to show stable underlying demand: ~90% membership renewal intent, modest single-digit revenue growth as the realistic 2026 baseline, and 79% customer acceptance of price increases (tied to perceived value). However, the report emphasizes two rising concerns: market saturation remains the #1 operator worry, and price sensitivity is increasing. Subscription growth rates have moderated compared to earlier years, signaling a more mature phase of membership adoption. The industry is pivoting from acquisition-led growth to operational excellence — conversion rates, per-site EBITDA, and churn management are the new battlegrounds.

Sources: carwash.org — CAR WASH Pulse Q1 2026, carwash.com, carwashpro.com

These numbers define the upper bound of what a membership-focused operator can achieve — and provide UCWH with concrete per-site benchmarks to measure against as it scales from 3 to 20+ locations.

👉 Whistle Express Divesting Utah = Strategic Lesson for UCWH's Expansion Planning

Strategic Insight

Whistle Express selling 13 Utah locations to Quick Quack is a telling strategic move. Despite being the largest express car wash operator (530 sites), Whistle chose to exit a market where a competitor (Quick Quack) had superior local density and brand awareness. This validates a core principle in UCWH's expansion planning: market density matters more than total footprint. Quick Quack's 70+ Utah sites make it the dominant local player — even the #1 national chain by count couldn't compete effectively. For UCWH, this reinforces the Houston-first expansion strategy: build density in your home market before spreading thin across geographies.

Recommended Action: Use the Whistle/Quick Quack Utah example in internal strategy discussions as evidence that density wins over breadth. Apply the 2-mile radius rule rigorously for Houston expansion — prioritize clustering new sites to create local market density (similar to how Tommy's has 6 El Paso locations and BlueWave has 11 San Antonio locations). A UCWH Houston cluster of 5-6 sites would create meaningful local density comparable to competitors, enabling shared marketing spend and membership portability convenience that a scattered 5-site footprint across multiple cities cannot match.
