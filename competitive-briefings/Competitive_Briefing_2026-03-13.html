🟣 Whistle Express

$385M Take 5 Acquisition Closed — Now Largest US Express Car Wash

Acquisition

Whistle Express completed its acquisition of Driven Brands' Take 5 Car Wash for $385M ($255M cash + $130M seller note), creating the largest express car wash company in the US with 530 locations across 23 states. Driven Brands used proceeds to reduce leverage, targeting 3x net debt by end of 2026. This was the largest deal in express car wash history.

Sources: BusinessWire, carwash.org, Driven Brands Investor Relations

New CEO Luke Byerly — Former Jiffy Lube President

Leadership

Whistle Express appointed Luke Byerly as CEO, replacing José Costa (who remains on the board). Byerly brings 20+ years of automotive, retail, and franchise leadership from Jiffy Lube International. His franchise operations expertise signals Whistle may pursue a more systematic growth and branding strategy as it integrates 530 locations under one platform.

Sources: carwash.com, whistleexpresscarwash.com

Colorado Market Launch — 20 Take 5 Sites Converting to Whistle

Expansion

Whistle Express launched in Colorado with 20 former Take 5 locations, the first large-scale market conversion post-acquisition. Grand openings featured two weeks of free car washes at each site. This is the playbook Whistle will use to integrate and rebrand hundreds of Take 5 sites across the country.

Sources: PR.com, whistleexpresscarwash.com

🟠 Zips Car Wash

Chapter 11 Restructuring — $654M Debt, 41 Leases Being Rejected

Bankruptcy

Zips Car Wash (Plano, TX) filed Chapter 11 in February 2025 with $654M in debt and just $1M in cash. The pre-negotiated plan reduces debt by $279M through a debt-for-equity swap backed by 100% of lenders. Key details: $375M in take-back debt ($150M HoldCo + $225M OpCo), new $15M revolver, $82.5M DIP financing, and lease rejections on 41 underperforming locations. Multiple sites have been sold to El Car Wash in Florida.

Sources: CRE Daily, TheStreet, Bondoro, Bloomberg Law

Post-Bankruptcy Acquisition: Jet Brite (11 Chicagoland Sites)

Acquisition

Despite the restructuring, Zips acquired Jet Brite Car Wash — 11 high-volume locations in the Chicagoland market plus 3 sites in development and access to Shadow Equipment. Amplify Car Wash Advisors facilitated the deal. This suggests the reorganized Zips intends to remain an active acquirer in attractive markets, making their exit from weaker markets more likely.

Source: Auto Laundry News (carwashmag.com)

📊 Industry Trends & Market Data

CAR WASH Pulse Q4 2025: Industry Braces for Slower 2026 Growth

Market Data

The International Carwash Association's Q4 2025 Pulse report signals that operators expect revenue growth to decelerate in 2026 versus 2025. Key drivers: subscription growth flattening, competition intensifying, and cost pressures persisting. Modest single-digit growth is the realistic baseline — favoring businesses that prioritize efficiency, differentiation, and disciplined investment over pure expansion plays.

Source: carwash.org — CAR WASH Pulse Q4 2025

Membership Economics: 10% YoY Growth, Churn Stabilizing at 7–8%

Membership

Membership revenue grew approximately 10% year-over-year in 2025, while churn stabilized near 7–8% — signaling a more mature, durable subscription model. Tiered unlimited wash programs are expected to grow 15–20% across North America in 2026. Top-performing operators report EBITDA margins of up to 50% on subscription revenue, and leading platforms convert 35% of visitors into unlimited members.

Sources: carwash.org, carwash.com, Grand View Research, Persistence Market Research

US Car Wash Market: $15.8B in 2026, Express Exterior Dominates

Market Data

The US car wash services market is expected to reach $15.75B in 2026 (up from $15.28B in 2025). Express Exterior Wash continues to dominate with 55% market share, processing 100+ vehicles/hour. The Southwest region leads with 45% market share driven by favorable weather, population growth, and year-round operations. Industry consolidation continues as ~900+ new sites open annually.

Sources: Grand View Research, Persistence Market Research, IBISWorld
🧠 AI Insights — What This Means for UCWH

🚨 UT426 Is Now Surrounded — Quick Quack Has 70+ Utah Locations

Critical

This is the most significant competitive development this week for UCWH. Quick Quack's acquisition of 13 Take 5 locations brings their Utah presence to 70+ sites — compared to UCWH's single UT426 in Saratoga Springs. QQ now has massive advantages in Utah: brand awareness saturation, membership cross-sell opportunities between locations, labor market leverage, and purchasing power. Your single site is competing against a chain that can afford to undercut on membership pricing, offer members more convenient wash locations, and attract employees with career paths across 70 sites.

Recommended Action: UT426 must differentiate on experience quality and community connection — areas where a single-operator franchise can outperform a large chain. Double down on the grassroots marketing playbook locally: school partnerships, youth sports sponsorships, neighborhood events. Your member health scoring system (usage 40%, payment 25%, engagement 20%, experience 15%) is a genuine competitive advantage — use it to reduce churn below the industry 7–8% benchmark. If UT426 economics are strong, this is also a signal to consider whether Utah expansion (second site) makes sense to build scale before QQ completely locks up the market.

💡 The Competitive Landscape Just Reshuffled — Know Your New Enemies

Strategic

The express car wash competitive hierarchy has fundamentally changed. Whistle Express (530 locations) is now #1, followed by MCW (500+, going private), Quick Quack (300+), Tommy's Express (270+), and Tidal Wave (310). Meanwhile, Zips (260, Chapter 11) is shedding locations, and BlueWave/ClearWater (81+) is growing aggressively in your Texas backyard. The industry is splitting into two tiers: massive-scale national operators (500+ sites) and focused regional platforms (50-300 sites). UCWH at 3 sites needs to think carefully about which competitors matter locally versus nationally.

Recommended Action: Update your competitive analysis framework to focus on LOCAL competitive density in your two markets. In Houston: Quick Quack (Astros deal, heavy presence), BlueWave/ClearWater ($370M war chest), and Tommy's corporate franchise pipeline are your primary threats. In Utah: Quick Quack (70+ sites) is the dominant concern. National chains like Whistle Express and Tidal Wave are secondary until they enter your specific trade areas. Use the 2-mile radius rule rigorously for every site selection decision.

⚠️ Slower 2026 Growth = Play Defense on Membership Retention

Strategic Priority

The ICA Pulse data shows the industry is shifting from "growth at all costs" to "efficiency and retention" in 2026. Membership revenue grew 10% in 2025, but operators expect that to flatten. Churn is stabilizing at 7–8% — right at your target threshold. In a slower-growth environment, every lost member costs more to replace because new member acquisition is getting harder (saturated markets, consumer fatigue on subscriptions). Your 75 new members/month target becomes harder if the market is decelerating.

Recommended Action: Shift marketing spend emphasis from pure acquisition to retention for Q2 2026. Your proactive member health scoring system is ahead of the curve — most operators don't have this. Use it aggressively: flag at-risk members early, trigger win-back campaigns before cancellation, and invest in the "surprise and delight" moments that build loyalty. Your 2% grassroots budget should emphasize existing member engagement (member appreciation days, referral bonuses) alongside new acquisition. Target: maintain ≤8% churn even as industry competition for members intensifies.

✅ Whistle Express CEO Change = Integration Distraction for a Major Competitor

Opportunity Window

Whistle Express just became the biggest player (530 locations) while simultaneously executing the largest acquisition in express car wash history AND replacing their CEO. That's three massive organizational disruptions at once. New CEO Luke Byerly has to integrate 530 locations from two different brands (Whistle + Take 5), establish his leadership culture, and deliver on the $385M investment thesis. History shows that post-merger integrations of this scale take 12–24 months and typically cause service quality disruptions, employee turnover spikes, and customer confusion during rebranding.

Recommended Action: If Whistle/Take 5 has locations in your Houston or Utah trade areas, now is the time to aggressively market against them. Rebranding creates customer uncertainty — "is my membership still valid? did the wash quality change?" — that you can capitalize on with targeted acquisition offers. Monitor Glassdoor and employee reviews at former Take 5 locations for signs of integration friction that could create hiring opportunities for UCWH.
