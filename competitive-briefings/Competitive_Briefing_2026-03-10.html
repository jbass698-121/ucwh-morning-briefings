🔵 Mister Car Wash

$3.1B Take-Private Deal with Leonard Green & Partners

Financial

Leonard Green & Partners will acquire all outstanding MCW shares it doesn't already own (~33%) for $7.00/share. The deal represents a 29% premium to the 90-day VWAP through Feb 17, 2026. LGP has been MCW's strategic partner since 2014. A Special Committee of independent directors unanimously recommended the deal. Expected to close H1 2026.

Sources: GlobeNewsWire, Yahoo Finance, MobilityPlaza

Multiple Shareholder Investigations Launched

Legal

At least five law firms — Johnson Fistel, Kessler Topaz, Levi & Korsinsky, BFA Law, and Halper Sadeh — have opened investigations into potential fiduciary duty breaches. The core concern: LGP used its controlling 67% stake to approve the sale to itself without soliciting votes from public stockholders, potentially undervaluing shares.

Sources: GlobeNewsWire, PRNewswire (multiple filings March 2–9)

Q4 Earnings: Revenue +4%, UWC Memberships +7%

Financial

In its Feb 18 earnings report, MCW posted 4% net revenue growth, 1.6% comp-store sales increase, 7% growth in Unlimited Wash Club memberships, and opened 16 new greenfield locations in the period. The new "Titanium" premium package achieved 15%+ penetration among new members.

Source: Mister Car Wash Investor Relations

📊 Industry Trends & Market Data

U.S. Car Wash Market: $1.4B (2026) → $2.1B (2033)

Market Data

The U.S. car wash market is projected to grow from $1.4B in 2026 to $2.1B by 2033, a 5.8% CAGR. Express Exterior Wash dominates with 55% market share, processing 100+ vehicles/hour. The Southwest region leads with 45% share driven by favorable weather, population growth, and year-round operations.

Source: Persistence Market Research

Subscription Adoption Driving Industry Economics

Membership

Leading car wash platforms now convert 35% of visitors into unlimited-wash members. Subscription revenue is the cornerstone of express car wash valuations, enabling predictable cash flows, rapid site clustering in high-traffic corridors, and unit-level EBITDA margins of 40–50%. Top chains average $1–2M in annual sales per location.

Sources: Car Wash Advisory, MMCG Invest

🚨 Quick Quack Is Making a Big Play in YOUR Houston Market

High Priority

The Houston Astros partnership puts Quick Quack front-and-center in the Houston metro — the same market where TX160 (FM 1960) and TX190 (Webster) operate. In-stadium activations, co-branded promos, and game-day specials will drive massive brand awareness among your exact customer demographic ($50K+ income households with vehicles). This is a direct competitive threat to your membership growth targets of 75 new paying members/month per site.

Recommended Action: Accelerate your local grassroots and community marketing strategy in Houston. Your 2026 plan already allocates 2% of revenue to grassroots — consider front-loading spend this quarter with hyper-local sponsorships (little league, school car wash fundraisers, Firestone-model partnerships) to counter QQ's brand push. You can't outspend a pro sports deal, but you can out-community it. Also explore whether Tommy's corporate has any co-op marketing funds available for competitive response.

💡 Quick Quack's Employee Ownership Should Be a Wake-Up Call

Talent Strategy

Your playbooks document ~200% annual employee turnover — double the already-high industry average — and your entire scaling strategy from 3 to 20+ sites depends on reducing this below 100%. Quick Quack just gave 4,200 team members equity in the company through a KKR-backed ownership program. This raises the bar for talent retention across the industry, especially in Texas where you directly compete for the same labor pool.

Recommended Action: Your site leader compensation is strong ($99K OTE) but frontline retention is the real vulnerability. Revisit your incentive system — could you add a tenure-based bonus, profit-sharing component, or equity-equivalent program as you scale? Even a simple "stay bonus" at 6- and 12-month marks could meaningfully reduce turnover while you build toward QQ-level retention programs. This is a strategic priority — you can't scale to 20 sites on a 200% turnover foundation.

✅ Your Membership Strategy Aligns with Industry Winners

Validation

The industry data this week validates your core strategy: leading platforms convert 35% of visitors to unlimited members, and subscription revenue drives 40–50% EBITDA margins at the unit level. Your targets (75 new members/month, ≤8% churn, 24+ month average duration) are right in line with what the best operators are achieving. MCW grew Unlimited Wash Club memberships 7% last quarter and their new Titanium premium tier hit 15% penetration — proof that tiered memberships with upsell paths work.

Recommended Action: Use MCW's Titanium tier launch as a case study for your own premium membership strategy. Your proactive member health scoring system (usage 40%, payment 25%, engagement 20%, experience 15%) is a competitive advantage most operators don't have — lean into it. Consider adding a premium tier with ceramic protectant, enhanced services, or priority access to differentiate from QQ's "Duck" tier model. This is where you can punch above your weight class against the 200+ location chains.
