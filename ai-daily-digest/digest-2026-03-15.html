Apple's Rebuilt Siri Ships This Month — Gemini-Powered, 10-Step Action Chains, On-Screen Awareness

Apple / Google / CNBC / Gadget Hacks · Mar 2026 · HIGH

Apple confirmed that iOS 26.4 will ship this month with a fundamentally rebuilt Siri powered by Google's 1.2 trillion parameter Gemini model running on Apple's Private Cloud Compute. The new Siri can chain up to 10 sequential actions from a single natural language request — booking a flight, adding it to a calendar, and texting an arrival time in one command. On-screen context awareness lets Siri read and reference whatever's currently displayed: a restaurant in Safari triggers a reservation, a flight confirmation in Mail gets auto-added to the calendar. Under a multiyear deal worth roughly $1B/year, Gemini is entirely white-labeled with no Google branding visible to users. Apple retains full control over UI, data routing, and privacy enforcement.

Why this matters for you: This is the moment agentic AI reaches 1.5 billion devices in consumer hands. Siri chaining 10 actions from a single command is functionally an agent — and it normalizes the concept of autonomous multi-step task completion for everyday users. For anyone building agent infrastructure or MCP connectors, Apple's move validates the core thesis and massively expands the addressable market. The white-label Gemini deal also signals that the "model layer" is becoming a commodity input — the value is in the orchestration, UX, and ecosystem integration on top.

Google Commits $1B to Form Energy's 100-Hour Battery — 30GWh Iron-Air System for Minnesota Data Center

TechBuzz / Canary Media / Heatmap News · Mar 2026 · HIGH

Google committed approximately $1 billion to Form Energy for a 300-megawatt, 30-gigawatt-hour iron-air battery system — the largest battery deployment in the world by energy capacity. The system will deliver continuous power for 100 hours straight, supporting a new Google data center in Minnesota alongside 1,400MW of wind and 200MW of solar. Form Energy's iron-air chemistry uses abundant, cheap materials (iron and air) rather than lithium, enabling multi-day storage at a fraction of lithium-ion costs. Battery delivery begins in 2028, with Form planning a substantial funding round in late 2026 before a 2027 IPO. The deal signals that hyperscaler AI energy demand is creating entirely new infrastructure categories beyond traditional power procurement.

Why this matters for you: AI's energy problem is now spawning billion-dollar infrastructure investments in technology that didn't exist at scale five years ago. Google committing $1B to a 100-hour battery signals that the hyperscalers are moving beyond "just buy more power" to fundamentally reshaping energy infrastructure. For AI-first businesses, the implication is that compute costs will increasingly be dominated by energy economics, not chip pricing. The iron-air chemistry's use of abundant materials (vs. scarce lithium) suggests this could scale more sustainably than current battery tech — a long-term tailwind for AI infrastructure affordability.
