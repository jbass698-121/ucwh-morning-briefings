MCP 2026 Roadmap: Stateful Sessions, Horizontal Scaling, and Service Discovery

MCP Blog · Mar 2026 · HIGH

The official MCP 2026 roadmap reveals the protocol's most critical production gaps. Streamable HTTP unlocked remote MCP servers, but running at scale has surfaced consistent problems: stateful sessions conflict with load balancers, horizontal scaling requires workarounds, and there's no standard way for a registry or crawler to discover what a server offers without connecting to it. The roadmap prioritizes these three gaps alongside enterprise auth integration and configuration portability -- moving MCP from "works in dev" to "works in production at scale."

Why this matters for you: These are exactly the problems you'd hit scaling your MCP-based pipeline. Service discovery means your orchestrators could automatically find and connect to new tool servers. Horizontal scaling support means your 2:00-6:00 AM batch window could parallelize more aggressively. And stateful session fixes would improve reliability for multi-step operations like your daily data pull that chains multiple MCP calls. Watch for these features landing -- they'll directly unblock pipeline optimization.
