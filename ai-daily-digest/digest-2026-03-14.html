Security & Governance

FTC AI Preemption Deadline Arrives — Federal vs. State AI Governance Showdown Begins

Baker Botts / IAPP / Mondaq · Mar 11 · HIGH

The March 11 deadline for the FTC to issue its AI policy statement on federal preemption of state laws has arrived, triggered by Trump's December 2025 executive order "Ensuring a National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence." The FTC was directed to describe when state AI laws requiring "alteration of truthful outputs" are preempted by federal deceptive-practices law. The Commerce Department was simultaneously directed to identify "burdensome" state AI laws for referral to the AI Litigation Task Force. Legal experts question the viability of the preemption theory, noting that FTC policy statements are interpretive rather than binding, and courts may reject the premise that correcting for bias constitutes deception. California's TFAIA and Texas's RAIGA — both effective January 1, 2026 — are the most prominent potential targets.

Why this matters for you: This is the most consequential AI regulatory moment of 2026 so far. If federal preemption succeeds, companies deploying AI nationwide would face one set of rules instead of a patchwork of 50 state laws — dramatically reducing compliance costs. But the legal theory is untested, and courts are likely to challenge it. For AI-first businesses, the practical advice is to continue building to the strictest state standards (California's TFAIA) while monitoring the federal preemption litigation. The outcome will determine whether AI governance centralizes federally or remains a state-by-state compliance maze.
