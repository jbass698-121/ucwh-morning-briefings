White House AI Framework Proposes Federal Preemption of State AI Laws

White House / Nextgov · Mar 20 · HIGH

The Trump administration's national AI legislative framework proposes preempting California's AI Transparency Act, Texas's Responsible AI Governance Act, and Colorado's AI Act -- all in various stages of taking effect. The framework recommends no new federal AI rulemaking body, maintaining a "sector-specific" approach. Colorado's AI Act takes effect June 30, creating a deadline pressure for Congressional action. Critics say it delivers Big Tech's top policy priority under an innovation banner; supporters argue it prevents compliance chaos.

Why this matters for you: The June 30 Colorado deadline creates a forcing function. If Congress doesn't act, businesses operating across state lines face a patchwork. For Parallax Holdings, the practical question is whether AI-generated reports, automated customer communications, or AI-driven business decisions trigger disclosure requirements. The definition of "high-risk AI" is currently different in every state. Monitor but don't over-invest in compliance until the federal vs. state question resolves.
