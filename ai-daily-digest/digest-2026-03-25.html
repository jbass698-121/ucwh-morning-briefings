Linux Foundation Unifies MCP and A2A Under New Agentic AI Foundation

Linux Foundation · Mar 2026 · HIGH

The Linux Foundation launched the Agentic AI Foundation (AAIF), co-founded by OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, AWS, and Block, as the permanent governance home for both MCP (agent-to-tool) and A2A (agent-to-agent) protocols. IBM's Agent Communication Protocol merged into A2A, and the foundation's charter covers transport scalability, enterprise auth, audit trails, and configuration portability. The protocol fragmentation era is over -- MCP and A2A are now the de facto standards under neutral governance.

Why this matters for you: This is the "HTTP moment" for AI agents. With MCP under Linux Foundation governance and all major players co-founding, your investment in MCP-based tool connections (Supabase, Slack, Gmail) is now built on the industry standard, not a single vendor's protocol. Future integrations -- connecting new data sources, adding tools, or enabling agent-to-agent workflows between your 18 tasks -- will follow these standardized protocols. Vendor lock-in risk for your agent infrastructure just dropped significantly.
